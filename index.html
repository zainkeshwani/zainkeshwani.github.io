PROJECTS

  • Making this website: as you may have read on the welcome page, this website was made to showcase who I am and what I have done coded from scratch in HTML/CSS/JS


  • Vector Calculator + Visualizer coded in Java and JavaFX. In my AP Physics class, there were a lot of vector calculations that needed to be done in order to get to a final answer. As a result of this, I made this calculator to help me in doing so. This calculator can do basic operations such as addition and subtraction, but also can find dot and cross products, the angle between two vectors, and much more. After performing these calculations, though, the visualizer allows you to draw the two vectors as well as the resultant to make it easier to see the operation's results.


  • Amazon Web Scraper in Python using BeautifulSoup and Selenium. This was something I did to make it quicker and easier to search for items on Amazon.com without having to actually open the website and look through all the search results. The program will take in an item name (what you are searching for), a maximum price you are willing to pay, as well as the number of results you wish to see and scrape the site to retrieve these items. It then will add the item you choose to the cart with a quantity and then open Amazon.com and show you your cart.


  • Matrix Calculator coded in Java. When I was creating my Vector Calculator (see above), I realized that more often than not, vectors are represented as matrices. This gave me the idea to create a calculator for matrices as well. The calculator supports a vast range of operations, from simple things such as addition or scalar multiplication, as well as more complex things like finding transposes, adjoints, and much more. In said program, I also made my own exceptions for times where calculations could not be performed due to preconditions (ex: adding two matrices of different orders or finding the inverse of matrices that have a determinant of zero).


  • Triangle Solver coded in Java. This program will take in a triangle based on input (ex: base and height, three sides, etc.) and output as much information as it can about the triangle. The program also has a subclass specifically for right triangles, as oftentimes there are different procedures used for them. This was something I found useful in class when, given limited information about a triangle, we were required to list as much as we could about the triangle based on the given info. In said program, I also made my own exceptions for times where calculations could not be performed due to preconditions (ex: trying to create triangles that cannot exist or attempting to create triangles with negative angles or side lengths).


  • Rational Function Information Finder coded in Java. I took AP Pre-Calculus in the 10th grade, where we learned all about rational functions in math. As such, I thought it would be a good idea to make a program that can simply give you all the information about the rational function. For example, my program will tell you the x and y intercepts of the function, simplify the rational and find all of its discontinuities (holes and vertical asymptotes), and much much more. All in all, this useful program helped me double check my work more often than not and also was a good way to practice applying my knowledge of Java to something useful in my everyday life.


  • Sentence and Word Check Project coded in Python to check if individual words are in the dictionary, as well as check if sentences have proper grammar (capitalization, punctuation, all words are spelled correctly, and more). The program, being linked to a free dictionary API online, can produce synonyms and antonyms for words that you input to it.


  • Magic 8 Ball coded in Java. Originally starting as a quick and easy side project to kill time, this actually evolved into a full scale project. I was introduced to JFrame as a result of this and was also able to have some fun by asking the 8 Ball random questions during testing!
